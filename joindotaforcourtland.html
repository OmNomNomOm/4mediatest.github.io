Four days after joining forces, Ah Fu and LFY qualify for their first LAN

posted by Abelle, 70 days ago
It looks like LGD.ForeverYoung's recent roster changes have already paid off with a win over their brother team, LGD, qualifying them for the upcoming EPICENTER: Moscow Season 2 LAN.




LGD.Forever Young defeats @LGDgaming in a tight match with 2-1 score.
Welcome to #EPICENTER: Moscow, LFY! pic.twitter.com/a9t1ykvFy1

— EPICENTER: Moscow (@epicentergg) May 10, 2017


LGD.Forever Young had been knocked into the lower bracket of the EPICENTER Chinese qualifier in the first round with a 2-1 loss to Vici Gaming J, but fought their way through the entire lower bracket, even beating VGJ 2-0 in a rematch, to reach the Grand Final.

LGD-Gaming, on the other hand, spent the entire qualifier in the upper bracket. They hadn't lost a single game until they met LFY in the final.

The series went to a full three games, but the two wins from LFY were very one-sided. In Game 1, LGD had only 5 kills compared to LFY's 29. Xie 'Super!' Junhao's Death Prophet balled out of control extremely quickly, and the rest of LFY's lineup had enough lockdown to deal with both Wang Chun 'Ame' Yu's Weaver and Yao 'Maybe' Lu's Ember Spirit.

LFY tried a similar draft in Game 2 with Super on Death Prophet and Du 'Monet' Peng on Troll Warlord for a second time, but LGD seemed much more comfortable the second time around and kept things from getting out of hand, tying things up with a win.

Despite the Game 2 adjustments, Game 3 was another disaster for LGD. LFY took the ever-popular Terrorblade as their carry and overwhelmed their opponents. The score was a bit less stompy than Game 1 (35-13 kills) but LGD never came close to a comeback.

With this win, LFY are headed to Moscow June 4th-11th to compete in the main event of EPICENTER: Moscow Season 2. They are the first qualifier team to be confirmed, as the rest of the regional qualifiers wrap up later this week, on May 14th.
