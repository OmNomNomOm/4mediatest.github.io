StarSeries: "Awful day" for Alliance who take only 1 point from an available 9

posted by Malystryx.GDS, 1 day ago
Alliance started the StarSeries Season 3 Qualifiers so well but in the space of 24 hours are now looking incredibly unlikely to progress to the LAN finals.






Rough day for Alliance who look to have lost any reasonable shot at 2nd place


For Team Secret and Natus Vincere the campaign is still young but for The Alliance it all fell apart today. The Swedes had started so well last week with a draw against Team Liquid and Cloud9 and victory over Horde giving them 5 points of an available 9.

They were in a position to secure their spot near the top of the table today with three back-to-back matches against Secret, Vega and Navi, but ended the day with two defeats and one draw. 1 point from an available 9.

Secret's new addition Maurice 'KheZu' Gutmann shone on Enigma in game 1 versus Alliance with some exquisite Blackholes and Pyo 'MentalProtector' Noa's Blink Dagger PA in game 2 made sure they were no survivors.

Alliance then moved on to play Vega and were involved in an 83 minute first game featuring 97 kills. Alliance held off Vega's attempts to take out a second set of rax for the last 20 minutes of the game thanks to diversions orchestrated by Simon 'Handsken' Haag's Pudge and the magical damage and survivability of Linus 'Limmp' Blomdin Octarine Core Ember Spirit. It was unfortunately not enough for Alliance who had definitely put in the effort to try to turn the game around.


awful day ??at least Denmark won csgo

— Linus Blomdin (@LimmpDota) 29 January 2017



The second game versus Vega ran for 43 minutes and although Alliance chalked up a lot of kills they did not secure enough objectives, with two of Vega's tier 2s still standing by the end of the game. Vega's draft in game 2 was a nasty one, using Underlord and Sand King in combination to set up an attack from Dmitriy 'Ditya-Ra' Minenkov's Chaos Knight and Sergey 'God' Bragin's Sniper Lone Druid (Learn more about Sniper Lone Druid in our guide). Alliance the moved on to their final series of the day against Navi.

jonassomfan's offlane Razor averaged 1 death every 3 minutes




Alliance's game 1 versus Navi was their worst performance of the day, most likely the consequence of losing their first 4 games despite playing relatively well. Game 1 lasted just 24 minutes after Alliance's Crystal Maiden and Chaos Knight support duo were unable to keep the obscure Jonas 'jonassomfan' Lindholm's offlane Razor alive, who suffered three deaths in the first 8 minutes of the game, too easy of a target for Per Anders 'Pajkatt' Olsson L.'s Lifestealer.

After their fifth loss in a row Alliance looked set to go 0-6 on their penultimate day of StarSeries matches, but they pulled together in the final game of the day to force a draw.

Limmp's cheeky Sniper in game 2 versus Navi


Alliance have a last hurrah against Navi


After a rough first game it was now Navi's turn to be on the receiving end of a beating, delivered by Linus 'Limmp' Blomdin's 766 GPM Sniper, who went 12-0 in Alliance's final game of the day. Limmp even cheekily lurked on the popular observer word spot near Radiant's Secret Shop to Snipe his prey. During the game Alliance even pulled off one of their trademarks, level 1 Roshan.

"Certain strats never die" - Alliance pull of level 1 Roshan vs Navi


Certain strats never die. pic.twitter.com/a3hWe2jkm2

The results today will be far from ideal for Alliance but at least they ended on a win, and they will play their last game of the qualifiers against Ad Finem on the 2nd February. For Navi, who could still challenge for top 2, there is clearly still work to be done on the new international roster, but it is early days yet for their new squad.

State of the group: Liquid could still be overtaken by Secret or Navi


Team Liquid's swift run through the StarSeries EU Qualifiers means they will finish in 1st place unless Team Secret and Natus Vincere can win all their remaining matches. Liquid did not lose any of their seven 2-game series, and with every single other team suffering at least one defeat it means for Secret and Navi to overtake them they would need to take all 15 points from their remaining 5 games.

The EU Qualifiers offers 2 slots at the Starladder i-League Season 3 Finals, and second place is still very open, although a few teams have made a solid start. Ad Finem, who made their first appearance since the Boston Major against Liquid last week, still look as strong on 7.0. However, Cloud9's 2-0 victory against Ad Finem and Vega Squadron also makes them a strong challenger for the second LAN spot.
