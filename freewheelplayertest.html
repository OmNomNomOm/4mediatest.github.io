LFY and Newbee advance, we are guaranteed a Chinese team in the TI7 Grand Final

posted by Abelle, 5 hours ago
The upper bracket matches of Day 3 had our jaws on the floor, and in the end both LFY and Newbee advance and will face each other in the Winner's Final.




Invictus Gaming vs. Newbee

Newbee struck first in the opening game of this seires between Chinese titans. Both teams went blow for blow for the majority of the game, but by the time the late-game rolled around, Sccc's Sniper was able to stand his ground and mow down every single iG hero.

Boboka picked up his team and threw them on his back in Game 2. His Earth Spirit plays around the map had his name on ODPixel and Fogged's lips more often than not, and Newbee's attempts to fight and engaged were constantly being foiled by a well placed Boulder Smash here or a Geomagnetic Grip there. iG's draft as a whole also did a great job of keeping down uuu9 / Moogy's Drow Ranger, who had a terrible time getting anything done in fights. After accruing a significant lead, iG tied the series by cutting down Newbee outside their base.

Sccc's Storm Spirit was the star of the show in the third and final game. After a slow start, Newbee started to pick up steam while Burning's Lycan was lagging behind in farm. Op / ???'s Tinker did a good job of slowing down Newbee's push, but the inevitable end came after 48 minutes.

Newbee outlast iG to reach the Upper Bracket Finals, one big step closer to bringing a second Aegis home for the organization. #TI7 pic.twitter.com/W7xgjAqyj1

— DOTA 2 (@DOTA2) August 10, 2017



Virtus.pro vs. LGD.Forever Young

VP's heavy team-fight oriented draft looked scary in their first game against LFY, but the Chinese squad did what some might expect from the CIS squad of the map, playing very aggresively early on. Du 'Monet' Peng's Morphling got off to an extremely hot start farming both creeps and heroes alike, getting him a very quick Linken's Sphere and Ethereal Blade. Virtus.pro called a very early GG after losing a fight in their own base at the 26 minute mark.

VP seemed better prepared for LFY's pace in Game 2, meeting it head on with aggression of their own. The game was extremely action-packed, with both squads preferring to be hunting for kills and objectives with their cores over sitting back and farming. Both teams had strong pushing heroes as well, so by the 30 minute mark each team had just one outer tower remaining.

The CIS squad's lead began to build with both no[o]ne's Viper and RAMZES666's Lycan holding significant net worth leads over LFY's cores. They confidently pushed LFY's high ground, but LFY patiently looked for an opening. When they finally found one, it paid off with a huge fight ending in a team wipe and a big momentum shift.

VP fought for their lives in what were the most insane final several minutes of a game on this main stage so far but LFY prevailed and knocked them down to the lower bracket after a 2-0 victory.
